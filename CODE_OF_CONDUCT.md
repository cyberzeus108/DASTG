DASTG is a humane, community-powered initiative.

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All contributors and maintainers
- All spaces associated with this project, including:
  - GitHub Issues and Pull Requests
  - Discussions and documentation
  - Any official DASTG online or offline gatherings

By participating in this project, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct.

## ‚úÖ Expected Behavior

All participants in the DASTG project are expected to:

1. **Be respectful and kind**
   - Use welcoming and inclusive language
   - Assume good intentions, but allow room for correction
   - Disagree with ideas without attacking people

2. **Listen to lived experience**
   - Respect the input of on-ground caregivers, feeders, and local residents
   - Be cautious about giving strong opinions on situations you have not experienced

3. **Communicate clearly**
   - Stay on topic in issues and PRs
   - Provide constructive feedback with specific suggestions
   - Ask for clarification instead of making assumptions

4. **Prioritize safety and ethics**
   - Recommend **humane, legal** practices only
   - Flag any content that may be harmful, unsafe, or cruel
   - Acknowledge uncertainty; it‚Äôs okay to say ‚ÄúI‚Äôm not sure‚Äù

5. **Respect boundaries**
   - Do not pressure others to share personal information
   - Do not demand unpaid labor or immediate responses

---

## ‚ùå Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are **not acceptable** in the DASTG community:

1. **Harassment or abuse**, including:
   - Personal attacks or insults
   - Hate speech, slurs, or derogatory comments
   - Threats of violence or intimidation

2. **Discrimination**, including:
   - Disparaging comments or exclusion based on:
     - Race, ethnicity, nationality
     - Gender, gender identity or expression
     - Sexual orientation
     - Religion or beliefs
     - Disability, health, or mental health
     - Economic or social background

3. **Deliberate cruelty toward animals**
   - Advocating for or describing **cruel practices** as acceptable
   - Encouraging **illegal or inhumane** methods
   - Sharing graphic content without clear, necessary context

4. **Disruption and bad-faith behavior**
   - Trolling, spamming, or persistent off-topic posting
   - Repeatedly derailing technical or operational discussions
   - Misrepresenting data, results, or your role in the project

5. **Violating privacy**
   - Doxxing or sharing private information without consent
   - Posting identifiable data about individuals without their permission

Maintainers and designated community moderators are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.

- A private or public **warning**
- Temporary or permanent **blocking** from:

- Proportionately to the situation
- With the goal of **protecting the community and the project**
